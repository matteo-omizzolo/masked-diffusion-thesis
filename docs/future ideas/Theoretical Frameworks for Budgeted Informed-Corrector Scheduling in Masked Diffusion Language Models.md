## Executive assessment

## Formalizing the problem

A faithful formulation is to let \(z_t\) denote the denoising state after predictor step \(t\), and \(a_t\in\{0,1\}\) indicate whether the informed corrector is applied at that step. With fixed predictor kernel \(P_t\) and fixed corrector kernel \(C_t\), the controlled dynamics are
\[
z_{t-1}\sim
\begin{cases}
P_t(z_t,\cdot), & a_t=0\\
C_t\!\circ P_t(z_t,\cdot), & a_t=1,
\end{cases}
\qquad \sum_{t=1}^T a_t \le B,
\]
and the objective is to maximize \(\mathbb E[Q(z_0)]\) for a final quality functional \(Q\). If you let actions depend on the evolving denoising state, the state can be augmented as \((z_t,b_t)\) where \(b_t\) is remaining budget; that is a standard finite-horizon constrained/budgeted control structure. If the scheduler is global and non-adaptive, the problem collapses to choosing a subset \(S\subseteq[T]\) with \(|S|\le B\) that maximizes the path functional \(F(S)=\mathbb E[Q(z_0)\mid S]\). Finite-horizon MDP theory supports both backward induction and LP formulations, and constrained/budgeted MDP theory adds exactly the sort of cost budget you described. ŅłĆciteŅłéturn34view0Ņłéturn21search0Ņłéturn21search2Ņłü

Why is this trajectory-level rather than step-level? Because the informed corrector is not an arbitrary local edit; in Zhao et al., it is a **Gibbs-inspired MCMC kernel** that uses model-derived conditional likelihoods to target low-confidence positions, fixes the mask configuration, and only changes non-mask tokens into other non-mask tokens. That means one corrector call modifies the state that all later predictor steps will see, so its value is mediated through downstream trajectory effects rather than an isolated per-step gain. The same paper explicitly motivates informed selection by prioritizing dimensions with lower conditional likelihood and notes that generation errors can persist and affect all future steps. ŅłĆciteŅłéturn20view0Ņłéturn20view1Ņłéturn20view2Ņłü

This also explains why simple separable rankings can fail even if the corrector itself is strong. A schedule variable \(a_t\) is valuable only through its interaction with the future path: whether later predictor steps amplify, preserve, or erase the intervention. Recent diffusion-LM papers independently point toward the same nonuniformity: ReMDM shows inference-time scaling from iterative revision; the model-scheduling paper finds middle denoising steps are most sensitive; and trajectory-refinement / SMC methods explicitly criticize myopic, stepwise optimization in favor of trajectory-level refinement. ŅłĆciteŅłéturn1view5Ņłéturn25search0Ņłéturn4view2Ņłéturn5view0Ņłéturn1view3Ņłü

A subtle but important modeling choice follows from this. If your thesis is really about ŌĆ£**which time indices** should receive the already-fixed corrector?ŌĆØ then a full POMDP may be unnecessarily broad. POMDP machinery becomes appropriate only if the scheduler must reason about latent future quality or hidden errors using noisy observations. If the decision is open-loop and budgeted, the better fit is not ŌĆ£POMDP first,ŌĆØ but **open-loop stochastic trajectory optimization**, with an adaptive MDP extension as a natural generalization. **What is the most principled existing theory for your problem?**  
The most principled theory is **finite-horizon budgeted stochastic control**, with an open-loop subset-selection reduction if the schedule is fixed before sampling. Unlike submodularity, it does not require diminishing returns; unlike bandits, it does not require decomposition into independent arms; and unlike graphical-model approximations, it does not require an assumed sparse factorization of schedule effects. **What would a strong theorem story look like under the main candidates?**  
For **finite-horizon budgeted control**, the strongest theorem story is: define the exact optimization; prove that if a chosen state summary is sufficient, the optimal scheduler satisfies Bellman recursion with remaining budget in the state; then give approximation bounds when the summary is only approximately Markov, or when the schedule value admits a bounded-order interaction decomposition. **Recommended best novel but plausible approach.**  
Use a **control-as-inference / FeynmanŌĆōKac approximation over schedules**.
