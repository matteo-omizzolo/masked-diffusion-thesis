\section*{Executive summary}

Phase~1 (ProSeCo-OWT, $N{=}50$, $T{=}64$) produced \emph{valid trajectory-level
measurements} ($\Delta_t$, $\eps$, $\eta_B$, $\gamma$) and an \emph{inadmissible
policy-comparison headline}. The $2026{-}04{-}19$ audit found two issues with
the original ``\texttt{entropy\_bot\_B} beats uniform by $+29\%$ at $B{=}8$'' claim:

\begin{enumerate}[leftmargin=1.4em]
  \item All policy rows were single-seed (Tier T4 under
    \texttt{ANALYSIS\_SPEC.md}~\S6), so no statistical basis for ranking.
  \item The reported gain mixed the additive surrogate $A(S){:=}\sum_{t\in S}\Delta_t$
    with the pipeline-evaluated joint gain $G(S)$. On the same data, $G(S)$ at
    $B{=}8$ has uniform${\approx}0.76$ and \texttt{entropy\_bot\_B}${\approx}0.08$
    (a $10{\times}$ \emph{loss}, not a $29\%$ win).
\end{enumerate}

Theorem~A's inequality holds under its three assumptions, but the bound
$2B\eps + 2\eta_B$ is \emph{empirically vacuous} on this system at every
$B{\in}\{4,8,16\}$ ($3.50$ vs plausible $G(\Sopt)\le 1.2$). The empirical and
theoretical responses --- Phase~2 paired sweep plus two candidate theorem
refinements --- are summarised below.

Phase~2b is in flight as HPC job $479581$; estimated completion late
$2026{-}04{-}19$.

\section{What changed (the audit)}

\paragraph{Methodology.} \texttt{ANALYSIS\_SPEC.md} pins:
$(\mathrm{i})$ paired estimator $\widehat{\Delta}_K(\pi, B) := G(\pi) - G(\text{uniform})$
on identical seeds, BCa bootstrap CI, $K{\geq}30$;
$(\mathrm{ii})$ Tier T1 (per-trajectory observable) / T2 (paired difference, CI
excludes 0) / T3 (paired, CI overlaps 0) / T4 (single-seed --- inadmissible);
$(\mathrm{iii})$ separate accounting of $A(S)$ and $G(S)$ with explicit naming.

\paragraph{Phase 1 disposition.} Trajectory-level measurements are Tier T1 and
remain the inputs to all theory; policy-comparison rows are Tier T4 and are
removed from the canonical headline. No Phase~1 numbers are deleted; the
chronological record is preserved with audit notes.

\section{What the theory now says}

\paragraph{Theorem A (unchanged statement).} Under
$(1)$~binary placement $k_t \in \{0,1\}$,
$(2)$~$\bigl|G(S) - \sum_{t\in S}\Delta_t\bigr| \le \eta_B$ for all $|S|\le B$,
$(3)$~$|\Delta_t - \psi(s_t)| \le \eps$ for all $t$,
\[
  G(\Sopt) - G(\Shat) \le 2 B \eps + 2 \eta_B .
\]
\textbf{Status:} \emph{proved under assumptions; empirically vacuous on the only
tested system} (ProSeCo-OWT, Phase~1).

\paragraph{Stress-test findings (\texttt{THEORY\_STRESS\_TEST.md}).}
\begin{itemize}[leftmargin=1.4em, itemsep=0pt]
  \item $\eta_{95}(B{=}8) = 0.68 \approx 0.9 \times $ true uniform $G$. Approximate
    additivity holds numerically but $\eta_B$ is large relative to the gain.
  \item $\eps_{\mathrm{rms}} \approx 0.134$ is small only because $\psi$ is nearly
    flat (Spearman${\approx}-0.19$). The uniform-$\eps$ bound conflates
    \emph{informative-low-$\eps$} and \emph{uninformative-low-$\eps$}.
  \item Proposition~C predicts $\eta_B \le \gamma{\cdot}B(B{-}1)/2 = 7.4$ at
    $B{=}8$ vs measured $0.68$ --- loose by ${\approx}11{\times}$. Pairwise
    interactions partially cancel.
  \item No operational definition of $\Sopt$ in the pipeline; the so-called
    ``oracle'' was top-$B$ of the mean-$\Delta$ profile (which equals $S_A^*$,
    not $\Sopt$). Phase~2b adds a Monte-Carlo lower bound on $G(\Sopt)$.
\end{itemize}

\paragraph{Candidate refinements (registered before Phase~2b).}
\begin{description}[leftmargin=1.4em, style=nextline, itemsep=0.2em]
  \item[Refinement~A$'$ (variance-form $\eta_B$).] Under pairwise interactions
    $\xi_{t,t'}$ with $\Eb\xi=0$ and $\mathrm{Var}\,\xi \le \sigma_\xi^2$,
    \[
      \Eb\bigl|G(S) - A(S)\bigr| \le \sigma_\xi\, B / \sqrt{2} ,
    \]
    so $\Eb[G(\Sopt) - G(\Shat)] \le 2 B\,\Eb\eps + 2 \sigma_\xi\, B/\sqrt{2}$.
    $\sqrt{B}$-tighter than Prop.~C and consistent with the measured
    $(\eta_{95}(B{=}4),\eta_{95}(B{=}8),\eta_{95}(B{=}16)) = (0.41, 0.68, 1.36)$.
    Status: \emph{plausible but incomplete} --- needs a mixing/cancellation
    hypothesis on $(\xi_{t,t'})$.
  \item[Refinement~A$''$ (rank-based $\eps_R$).] Define
    $\eps_R := (1 - |\mathrm{Spearman}(\psi, \Delta)|) \cdot \sigma_\Delta$. Under
    a Gaussian heuristic, $\Eb[A(S_A^{*}) - A(\Shat)] \le B \eps_R$, with
    $\eps_R = 0$ at $\rho = \pm 1$ and $\eps_R = \sigma_\Delta$ at $\rho = 0$.
    Isolates the rank-information content that Top-$B$ selection actually uses.
    Status: \emph{heuristic only}.
  \item[Negative-result corollary.] On $(\text{ProSeCo-OWT},\, T{=}64,\,
    F{=}-\mathrm{NLL}_{\mathrm{GPT2}})$, the measured $(\eps, \eta_B, \gamma)$
    imply $2B\eps + 2\eta_B > G(\Sopt)$ for all $B\in\{4,8,16\}$. Becomes a
    thesis-grade negative result if accompanied by a characterisation of regimes
    where $\eta_B/G \ll 1$ and at least one positive experiment in such a regime.
\end{description}

\paragraph{Combining-step bookkeeping.} The chain $G \!\to\! A \!\to\! A \!\to\! G$
yields $2B\eps + 2\eta_B$ when $\eta_B := \sup_{|S|\le B}|G(S) - A(S)|$, and
$2B\eps + 3\eta_B$ if the two $G$-applications are decoupled. Either is fine;
the LaTeX write-up must pin a definition.

\section{Phase 2 plan (\texttt{NEXT\_PHASE\_EXPERIMENT\_PLAN.md})}

Three gated stages.

\paragraph{Phase 2a --- offline reanalysis (no HPC).} Re-analyse the Phase~1
\texttt{protocol\_b} data under \texttt{ANALYSIS\_SPEC}. Deliverables:
$A{\leftrightarrow}G$ Spearman per $B$ (with bootstrap CI), $\sigma_\xi$ for
Refinement~A$'$, $\eps_R$ per signal. Decision gate: do mean-profile policies
belong in Phase~2b at all?

\paragraph{Phase 2b --- HPC paired sweep (in flight).} See \S4 below.

\paragraph{Phase 2c --- MAUVE F-swap (gated).} If any $(\pi, B)$ Phase~2b
margin sits within a $2\sigma_F$ noise band of zero, swap $F$ to a
pool-level MAUVE on the borderline subset and rerun. Cost cap: $8$ GPU-hours.

\section{What the thesis can and cannot say (today)}

\paragraph{Can say (Tier T1, audit-survived).}
\begin{itemize}[leftmargin=1.4em, itemsep=0pt]
  \item ProSeCo-OWT corrector is active: $61/64$ steps with
    $\bar\Delta_t > 0$, peak $\bar\Delta \approx 0.157$ at $t\approx 23$.
  \item Scheduling is potentially valuable: mean-field upper-envelope $G$ is
    $\approx 3.3{\times}$ uniform at $B{=}8$ (true upper envelope to be
    measured by Phase~2b MC oracle).
  \item Approximate additivity is quantitatively weak on this system:
    $\eta_{95}(B{=}8) \approx 0.9 \times G(\text{uniform})$.
  \item Proxy calibration $\eps_{\mathrm{rms}}$ is small \emph{because $\psi$ is
    flat}, not because $\psi$ is informative (Spearman${\approx}-0.19$).
\end{itemize}

\paragraph{Cannot say (yet).}
\begin{itemize}[leftmargin=1.4em, itemsep=0pt]
  \item Any signal-adaptive policy beats uniform on pipeline $G$.
  \item Theorem~A's bound is non-vacuous on any tested system.
  \item The ``entropy-minimum scheduling principle'' is a finding (it is a
    \emph{hypothesis} pending Phase~2b paired evidence).
\end{itemize}
