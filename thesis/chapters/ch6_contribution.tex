\section{Model-Agnostic Setup}
\label{sec:ch6-setup}

Fix a predictor schedule for a masked diffusion language model, an informed
corrector, a trajectory-level quality functional $F$, and a placement budget
$B$. The framework is model- and corrector-agnostic: the theory in this
chapter is independent of the particular corrector implementation.
ProSeCo-OWT is the primary empirical case study, not an assumption or scope
restriction of the theorem statements.

Let the denoising steps be $t \in \{1,\ldots,T\}$. A corrector schedule is a
set $S \subseteq \{1,\ldots,T\}$ with $|S|=B$, with at most one corrector
placement per step. The placement budget is $B$; the corresponding extra
forward-pass budget is
\[
  B_{\mathrm{NFE}} = c_{\mathrm{corr}} B,
\]
where $c_{\mathrm{corr}}$ is the number of extra forward passes induced by one
corrector placement. Thus all schedules with the same $B$ have the same
placement cost.

Let $y^S$ be the final sample produced with corrector schedule $S$, and let
$y^\emptyset$ be the corrector-free baseline. Define the schedule gain
\[
  G(S) := F(y^S) - F(y^\emptyset),
\]
the single-step marginal gain
\[
  \Delta_t := G(\{t\}),
\]
and the additive surrogate
\[
  A(S) := \sum_{t \in S} \Delta_t.
\]
The exact oracle in theorem statements is
$S_B^* \in \argmax_{|S|=B} G(S)$. In experiments this is not observed.
Empirical ``MC-oracle'' headroom means best-of-$N$ sampled schedules under
paired common random numbers; it is a pool estimate, not the exhaustive
${T \choose B}$ oracle.

For $t \ne t'$, define the operational second difference
\[
  \xi_{t,t'} := G(\{t,t'\}) - \Delta_t - \Delta_{t'}.
\]
The pairwise surrogate is
\[
  Q(S) := \sum_{t \in S} \Delta_t
       + \sum_{\substack{t<t'\\t,t'\in S}} \xi_{t,t'}.
\]
Here $Q(S)$ is a schedule-level surrogate. It must not be confused with the
quality-mass trajectory signal, written $QM_t$ in this thesis; historical raw
files may use the key \texttt{Q\_t} for $QM_t$.

Let $R_t$ be the corrector's action or revisable set at step $t$. Any signal
used to rank or condition a correction decision, such as entropy, inverse
margin, or $QM_t$, must be computed over the same $R_t$ on which the corrector
acts.

\section{Theorem A: Marginal Baseline}
\label{sec:ch6-theorem-a}

\begin{assumption}[Binary placement]
\label{assum:ch6-a1}
Schedules use exactly $B$ binary placements: $k_t \in \{0,1\}$ and
$\sum_t k_t = B$.
\end{assumption}

\begin{assumption}[Approximate additivity]
\label{assum:ch6-a2}
There is $\eta_B \ge 0$ such that
\[
  |G(S)-A(S)| \le \eta_B
  \qquad\text{for all } |S|\le B .
\]
\end{assumption}

\begin{assumption}[Proxy calibration]
\label{assum:ch6-a3}
There is a separable score $\psi_t$ and $\varepsilon \ge 0$ such that
\[
  |\Delta_t-\psi_t| \le \varepsilon
  \qquad\text{for all } t .
\]
\end{assumption}

Let $\widehat S_B$ be the set of the $B$ largest proxy scores $\psi_t$.

\begin{theorem}[Theorem A; marginal proxy regret]
\label{thm:ch6-a}
Under Assumptions~\ref{assum:ch6-a1}--\ref{assum:ch6-a3},
\[
  G(S_B^*) - G(\widehat S_B) \le 2B\varepsilon + 2\eta_B .
\]
\end{theorem}

\begin{lemma}[Top-$B$ under exact additivity]
\label{lem:ch6-a1}
If $A(S)=\sum_{t\in S}\Delta_t$ and $|S|=B$, then any set of the $B$ largest
$\Delta_t$ maximizes $A(S)$.
\end{lemma}

\begin{proof}
If a selected index $t$ has lower $\Delta_t$ than an unselected index $t'$,
exchange $t$ for $t'$. The value of $A$ cannot decrease. Repeating this
exchange leaves only $B$ largest-$\Delta_t$ indices.
\end{proof}

\begin{lemma}[Proxy top-$B$ regret]
\label{lem:ch6-a2}
If $|\Delta_t-\psi_t|\le\varepsilon$ for all $t$ and $\widehat S_B$ is top-$B$
by $\psi$, then
\[
  A(S_A^*) - A(\widehat S_B) \le 2B\varepsilon ,
\]
where $S_A^* \in \argmax_{|S|=B} A(S)$.
\end{lemma}

\begin{proof}
Pair elements of $S_A^*\setminus\widehat S_B$ with elements of
$\widehat S_B\setminus S_A^*$. For each pair $(o,s)$, top-$B$ optimality gives
$\psi_s\ge\psi_o$, hence
$\Delta_o \le \psi_o+\varepsilon \le \psi_s+\varepsilon
\le \Delta_s+2\varepsilon$. Summing over at most $B$ pairs proves the claim.
\end{proof}

\begin{proof}[Proof of Theorem~\ref{thm:ch6-a}]
By Assumption~\ref{assum:ch6-a2},
\[
  G(S_B^*) - G(\widehat S_B)
  \le A(S_B^*) - A(\widehat S_B) + 2\eta_B .
\]
Since $S_A^*$ maximizes $A$, $A(S_B^*)\le A(S_A^*)$. Lemma~\ref{lem:ch6-a2}
then gives $A(S_B^*)-A(\widehat S_B)\le 2B\varepsilon$.
\end{proof}

\paragraph{Role.}
Theorem~\ref{thm:ch6-a} is the baseline theorem. It says when informed timing
is reducible to marginal signal ranking. If additivity or proxy calibration
fails at the selected-schedule level, the theorem becomes a diagnostic
baseline rather than an operative scheduler guarantee.

\section{Diagnostics A$'$ and A$''$}
\label{sec:ch6-a-diagnostics}

Diagnostics A$'$ and A$''$ are empirical summaries, not regret theorems.
They do not control selected-schedule regret unless converted into a uniform
or finite-pool statement.

\paragraph{Diagnostic A$'$: additivity scale.}
For a schedule distribution $D_B$ or a fixed candidate pool $C_B$, report
\[
  \eta^{\mathrm{typ}}_{B,D}
  := \mathbb{E}_{S\sim D_B}|G(S)-A(S)|,
  \qquad
  \sigma^{\mathrm{pool}}_{\xi,B}
  := \operatorname{sd}_{S\in C_B}(G(S)-A(S)).
\]
These quantities measure typical additivity error on the sampled schedules.
They are not upper bounds on the regret of a selected top-$B$ schedule.

\paragraph{Diagnostic A$''$: rankability.}
Report rank correlations such as
\[
  R_B := \rho_{\mathrm{Spearman}}(A(S),G(S))
\]
over a fixed schedule sample. This indicates whether the additive surrogate
ranks schedules well on that sample. It is not a theorem constant and does not
by itself rule out failure on the selected schedule.

\section{Finite-Pool Selected-Schedule Form}
\label{sec:ch6-finite-pool-a}

The safe selected-schedule form of the marginal baseline is the finite-pool
special case of Theorem~B$'$ with $Q:=A$. For a fixed no-leakage candidate
pool $C_B$, define
\[
  \eta_{B,C} := \sup_{S\in C_B}|G(S)-A(S)|,
  \qquad
  \varepsilon_{B,C,\delta}
  := \sup_{S\in C_B}|A(S)-\widehat A(S)|
\]
on the event where the estimated additive surrogate $\widehat A$ is uniformly
accurate over $C_B$. If $\widehat S$ approximately maximizes $\widehat A$ over
$C_B$ with optimization gap $\omega_B$, then, with the probability attached
to the estimation event,
\[
  G(S_C^*) - G(\widehat S)
  \le 2\eta_{B,C} + 2\varepsilon_{B,C,\delta} + \omega_B,
\]
where $S_C^*\in\argmax_{S\in C_B}G(S)$ is the pool oracle. Comparison to the
full unobservable oracle requires the extra pool-approximation term
$\kappa_B=G(S_B^*)-G(S_C^*)$.

\section{Empirical Ranker-Class Limitation}
\label{sec:ch6-ranker-limitation}

\paragraph{Formal scope.}
In the seed-averaged additive problem with
$\bar\Delta_t := \mathbb{E}_i[\Delta_{i,t}]$, any time-only separable ranker
is bounded by the mean-profile top-$B$ envelope, because the best such
time-only additive schedule is exactly top-$B$ by $\bar\Delta_t$.

\paragraph{Empirical scope.}
On the ProSeCo-OWT case study, the tested separable rankers do not recover the
best-of-$N$ MC-oracle headroom, and the mean-$\bar\Delta_t$ envelope enters the
no-detectable-gain band by $B=8$ in the existing April 2026 results. This is a
scoped empirical limitation of the tested ranker class, not a universal theorem
over all feature-conditioned rankers.

\paragraph{Not ruled out.}
This limitation does not rule out feature-conditioned separable rankers outside
the tested class, non-separable uses of quality signals, pairwise schedulers,
online controllers, or search procedures using true-$G$ feedback. In
particular, a deployable scheduler claim requires Level~3 held-out evidence,
not merely population or true-$G$ search evidence.

\section{Theorem B: Pairwise Surrogate Framework}
\label{sec:ch6-theorem-b}

\begin{assumption}[Pairwise approximation]
\label{assum:ch6-b2}
There is $\zeta_B\ge0$ such that
\[
  |G(S)-Q(S)|\le \zeta_B
  \qquad\text{for all } |S|\le B .
\]
\end{assumption}

\begin{theorem}[Theorem B; exact surrogate]
\label{thm:ch6-b-exact}
Let $S_Q^*\in\argmax_{|S|=B}Q(S)$, and suppose $\widehat S$ has surrogate
optimization gap
\[
  Q(S_Q^*)-Q(\widehat S)\le \omega_B .
\]
Under Assumption~\ref{assum:ch6-b2},
\[
  G(S_B^*) - G(\widehat S) \le 2\zeta_B+\omega_B .
\]
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
Use $G(S_B^*)\le Q(S_B^*)+\zeta_B\le Q(S_Q^*)+\zeta_B$ and
$G(\widehat S)\ge Q(\widehat S)-\zeta_B
\ge Q(S_Q^*)-\omega_B-\zeta_B$.
\end{proof}

\begin{assumption}[Estimated surrogate]
\label{assum:ch6-b3}
There is $\alpha_B\ge0$ such that
\[
  |Q(S)-\widehat Q(S)|\le \alpha_B
  \qquad\text{for all } |S|\le B .
\]
\end{assumption}

\begin{theorem}[Theorem B; estimated surrogate]
\label{thm:ch6-b-estimated}
If Assumptions~\ref{assum:ch6-b2} and~\ref{assum:ch6-b3} hold and
$\widehat S$ approximately maximizes $\widehat Q$ with optimization gap
$\omega_B$, then
\[
  G(S_B^*) - G(\widehat S)
  \le 2\zeta_B + 2\alpha_B + \omega_B .
\]
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
The uniform two-sided error converts the $\widehat Q$ optimization gap into a
$Q$ optimization gap of at most $\omega_B+2\alpha_B$. Apply
Theorem~\ref{thm:ch6-b-exact}.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[Theorem B$'$; finite pool and high probability]
\label{thm:ch6-b-prime}
Fix a no-leakage candidate pool $C_B\subseteq\{S:|S|=B\}$. Suppose that
\[
  \zeta_{B,C}:=\sup_{S\in C_B}|G(S)-Q(S)|
\]
is finite, and with probability at least $1-\delta$,
\[
  \sup_{S\in C_B}|Q(S)-\widehat Q(S)|\le \alpha_{B,\delta}.
\]
Let $S_C^*\in\argmax_{S\in C_B}G(S)$ and let $\widehat S\in C_B$
approximately maximize $\widehat Q$ over $C_B$ with gap $\omega_B$. Then,
with probability at least $1-\delta$,
\[
  G(S_C^*)-G(\widehat S)
  \le 2\zeta_{B,C}+2\alpha_{B,\delta}+\omega_B .
\]
Against the unobservable full oracle,
\[
  G(S_B^*)-G(\widehat S)
  \le \kappa_B+2\zeta_{B,C}+2\alpha_{B,\delta}+\omega_B ,
\]
where $\kappa_B:=G(S_B^*)-G(S_C^*)$.
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
Restrict the estimated-surrogate proof to $C_B$. The term $\kappa_B$ is the
definition of the gap between the full oracle and the pool oracle.
\end{proof}

\paragraph{No-leakage caveat.}
The candidate pool $C_B$ must be fixed independently of held-out evaluation
$G$. Random MC pools, training-seed beams, and training-seed $\widehat Q$
candidates are admissible. A pool selected with held-out true-$G$ feedback is
not admissible for a held-out regret claim.

\paragraph{Level of claim.}
Theorem~B can be instantiated at three levels: seed-wise diagnostic,
population schedule, and feature-conditioned held-out scheduling. Only the
feature-conditioned Level~3 form can support a deployable scheduler claim.

\section{Diagnostic Framework C}
\label{sec:ch6-framework-c}

Diagnostic Framework C is a protocol, not a theorem. For a fixed
$(\mathrm{model},\mathrm{corrector},F,B)$ triple and a pre-specified schedule
pool, report:
\[
\begin{aligned}
  U_B^{MC,N} &:= G(S_B^{MC,N}) - G(S_B^{\mathrm{uniform}}),\\
  U_B^{pool} &:= G(S_C^*) - G(S_B^{\mathrm{uniform}}),\\
  R_B &:= \rho_{\mathrm{Spearman}}(A(S),G(S)),\\
  I_B &:= \operatorname{sd}(G(S)-A(S))/(\operatorname{sd}(A(S))+\delta_0),\\
  P_B &:= \rho_{\mathrm{Spearman}}(Q(S),G(S)).
\end{aligned}
\]
$U_B^{MC,N}$ is best-of-$N$ MC-oracle headroom; $U_B^{pool}$ is the oracle on
the fixed candidate pool; $U_B^*$ is reserved for the unobservable exhaustive
oracle and is not reported as an empirical quantity.

The intended regime map is:
\begin{itemize}
  \item no-op: no positive MC/pool headroom;
  \item marginal/rankable: $R_B$ high and tested rankers recover headroom;
  \item interaction-driven: pairwise predictability improves over additive
    predictability, e.g. $P_B>R_B$ and $\zeta_{B,C}<\eta_{B,C}$;
  \item higher-order or chaotic: headroom exists, but neither $A$ nor $Q$
    predicts schedules well;
  \item online-decision-like: offline structure exists and a compact online
    state supports a held-out budget-aware policy.
\end{itemize}

All classifications are statistical decisions with uncertainty over seeds and,
when relevant, over pair or schedule sampling. Pair heatmaps are diagnostics;
they do not by themselves validate Theorem~B. Schedule-level validation of
$Q(S)\approx G(S)$ on actual size-$B$ schedules is required.

\section{Appendix-Grade Objects}
\label{sec:ch6-appendix-grade}

\paragraph{Theorem D.}
The online budgeted controller abstraction is a standard finite-horizon
approximate dynamic programming statement. If an approximate value function is
uniformly within $\delta$ of the true value function over reachable states,
the greedy policy has loss at most $2T\delta$. This is appendix-grade unless a
compact observed state yields a statistically positive held-out scheduler.

\paragraph{Lemma E.}
The burn-in lemma is valid only for a clipped or bounded-Lipschitz surrogate
$F_C$. The experimental $F=-$GPT-2 NLL is not assumed to be Lipschitz in
Hamming distance. In the main text, burn-in exclusion should be justified by
measured $R_t$ and measured $\Delta_t$, not by an unconditional Lipschitz
claim.

\section{Claim Boundaries}
\label{sec:ch6-claim-boundaries}

\begin{itemize}
  \item Theorem~A is a marginal/ranker baseline under explicit assumptions.
  \item Diagnostics A$'$ and A$''$ report additivity scale and rankability;
    they are not unconditional regret refinements.
  \item Theorem~B/B$'$ is the central surrogate-regret framework, conditional
    on measured pairwise approximation and estimation assumptions.
  \item Diagnostic Framework C classifies regimes; it does not assert
    universality.
  \item MC-oracle headroom is best-of-$N$ pool headroom, not the exhaustive
    oracle.
  \item A deployable scheduler claim requires Level~3 feature-conditioned
    held-out evidence.
  \item Theorem~D and Lemma~E remain appendix-grade unless later empirical
    gates justify promotion.
\end{itemize}
